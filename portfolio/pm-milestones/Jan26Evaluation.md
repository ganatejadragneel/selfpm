## Part 3: Gaps (Brutal Honesty)

### 1. Scaling / Generalization (C)
You've built a product for one user: yourself. There's no evidence you've validated whether your framework applies to anyone else. "Identity Pressure" might be your unique pattern, not a generalizable insight.

**The Gap**: Product management at scale requires understanding diverse user needs, segmentation, and prioritization across conflicting requirements. You've optimized for n=1.

### 2. Technical Depth (B-)
You're using Claude Code to build features, which is fine. But your technical understanding appears shallow. When I explained git concepts, you needed basic definitions. Most AI PM roles at Anthropic expect you to deeply understand model training, inference, fine-tuning, RAG, etc.

**The Gap**: AI PM frameworks for 2026 require understanding of neural networks, NLP, supervised/unsupervised learning, and agentic frameworks. Your technical vocabulary seems limited.

### 3. Cross-Functional Leadership (?)
All your work is solo. There's no evidence of stakeholder management, engineering team leadership, design collaboration, or navigating organizational complexity.

**The Gap**: Anthropic PM roles explicitly require "cross-organizational collaboration, working through trade-offs, and balancing competing priorities."

**UPDATE**: Your Cardinality performance review shows strong cross-functional skills (4-5 ratings on teamwork, coaching, interpersonal communication). This gap may not exist - it just wasn't visible in your SelfPM portfolio.

### 4. Metrics That Matter for Business (C)
Your metrics are about personal behavior change. You haven't demonstrated understanding of business metrics: retention, activation, monetization, funnel optimization, A/B testing at scale.

**The Gap**: Anthropic's Consumer Growth PM role requires "experience with A/B testing and user funnel optimization techniques."

### 5. Shipping Under Constraints (?)
You've shipped features, but with no deadline pressure, no resource constraints, no competing priorities. Real PM work is about making hard tradeoffs with limited resources.

**UPDATE**: Your Cardinality work shows you ship under constraints (config sessions, sprint prep, multi-module ownership). This is evidence.

### 6. Market/Competitive Analysis (F)
Zero evidence of competitive analysis, market research, or understanding where SelfPM sits in the landscape of habit trackers, mental health apps, or self-quantification tools.

**The Gap**: PMs need to understand the market, not just the user.
